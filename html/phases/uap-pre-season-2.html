UAP Pre-season 2

Schedule & Workout Logs

Workout log (pdf)

This is the second phase of preseason work. The main goals are strength and power endurance. We are going to accomplish these goals primarily using circuit training. You'll do a power circuit twice per week and a strength circuit once per week. Workouts will be very short but very intense.

The SAQ work will be done before the conditioning workouts this week. This is mostly for maintenance and don't expect to see big gains in speed this phase.

General Principles

  • Learn to pay attention to how your body is feeling each day. If you start feeling run down by the end of each week, you may not be getting enough recovery. It is okay NOT to do all of the workouts provided. Sometimes less is more.
  • If you feel nauseated or dizzy, increase the rest periods. Making yourself vomit will not help the speed of your adaptation.

For this phase we have a split in training, depending on what surface your season is focussed on…

  • If GRASS –> use the standard Conditioning & SAQs
  • If BEACH –> use the Beach Conditioning & SAQs

Core circuits can be done 3-6 days per week. Alternate between circuit 1 and circuit 2.

Conditioning can be done 6 days per week but only if you keep the volume low enough to recover between sessions. *If you want to do conditioning on the same day as the circuit training, separate the workouts by a few hours*. This will allow you to do more work and still recover.

Strength

A1. Semi-supinated grip Pull-ups

https://www.youtube.com/embed/R-j7IvquHwE

  • "semi-supinated" means that your palms are facing one another. If you do not have a bar that allows for this, use a supinated grip (palms toward you)
  • keep your chest up. Squeeze shoulder blades together at the top of the movement.
  • If you do not have an assisted chinup machine, you can use a lat pulldown instead.

A2. Bodyweight (BW) Squats

https://www.youtube.com/embed/IpkzKC2mt7c

  • Get below parallel on each rep
  • Arms are extended to help maintain balance and keep weight on heels

B1. Bench Dips

https://www.youtube.com/embed/XwXGdC0K050

  • You should feel some tension in the pectorals at the bottom of the movement
  • Keep elbows parallel, not caving in or flaring out

B2. 45 Degree Lunges (BW)

https://www.youtube.com/embed/o9MgO2kMiAM

  • Step out at a 45 degree angle while keeping hips square
  • Get low but do not let the back knee hit the ground

C1. Pushups

https://www.youtube.com/embed/7lHuWdR6iQs

  • Maintain perfect plank position
  • Can be done on your knees if you cannot complete all as regular pushups

C2. Diagonal Plate Raise

https://www.youtube.com/embed/gbX8ipoRB7s

  • Start in a squat position with the plate held to one side.
  • Lift the plate diagonally and up over the opposite shoulder.
  • Your eyes should follow the plate during the movement.

D1. Plank Step-Up

https://www.youtube.com/embed/wiqVGrgaV4Q

  • Maintain perfect alignment of shoulders hips and ankles
  • Walk onto and off of the step as quickly as possible

D2. Scissor Jumps for height

https://www.youtube.com/embed/Bo_Rg5fhZMQ

  • Aim for maximum height
  • Feet should be shoulder width apart when landing.
  • With minimal ground contact time, jump up again into the next scissor.

Power

For the power circuit, exercises should be done as quickly as possible while maintaining good form. Keep your mental focus during this workout and get at it! **

A1. Single Arm DB High Pull

https://www.youtube.com/embed/qagm6U0CDLA

  • It's all in the hips.
  • DB should follow a straight line trajectory upwards
  • Switch arms at the 15 second mark

A2. DB High Pull to Press

https://www.youtube.com/embed/I-jYhdJqAPk

  • Catch the weight at the shoulder in a semisupinated position
  • Push the weight up as you stand up from a quarter squat position

A3. Squat to Press

https://www.youtube.com/embed/1O8jb9KLtf4

  • Find a rhythm and move quickly
  • Aim for parallel in the squats
  • The press should move continuously with momentum coming from the squat

Alternate between following this circuit up with either:

Day 1. Backward Lunges

https://www.youtube.com/embed/BYbEvMbOfMI

  • Step back with one leg. Do not let your knee touch the ground
  • Explosively step forward again using primarily the front leg
  • Keep the back foot off the ground when you stand up again if possible
  • Do all reps on one leg first and then the other

Day 2. Russian Step Ups

https://www.youtube.com/embed/jSnoZlYUttg

  • find a rhythm, be explosive, drive the knee up!
  • Use the foot on the bench to initiate and drive the movement. Don't "bounce" off the foot landing on the ground.

SAQ 1: Multi-directional

A. Lateral Bound and Return

https://www.youtube.com/embed/gxkZIPJYgro

  • Like the Bound and Stick, but with an extra bound back to the starting point.
  • Stick the landing as with the bound and stick

B. Heiden Hops

https://www.youtube.com/embed/u2e262ks7aY

C. 5-10-5 lateral drill

https://www.youtube.com/embed/lIq87ZhL8bE

  • First 5 yards hips will be mostly perpendicular to line of movement. Use a big crossover push.
  • Second 10 yards open the hips early coming in to the second turn
  • Third five yards turn the hips and commit to accelerating though the cone.

SAQ 2: Linear and Disc

A. Galloping

  • Repeatedly leap off the same leg as high and as far as possible
  • Use your arms for momentum

B. Power Skips for Distance

  • Aim for full hip extension and distance

C. Forward Frog Jumps

https://www.youtube.com/embed/nhDXP6h33qM

  • the purpose of this drill is to get full extension of ankles, knees, and hips
  • propel yourself explosively upward and a little bit forward

Core 1

A1. Deadbug

https://www.youtube.com/embed/Lbod9T8LvJc

  • Maintain natural curve in the spine
  • Move slowly with core conrtansly enganged

A2. Flat Lateral extension

https://www.youtube.com/embed/dmF-RPmsN6g

  • This position is most easily obtained with a partner sitting on your feet. I have also done it hooking my lower foot under a 60 pound dumbbell on the bench.
  • Maintain constant tension in the obliques as you lower yourself toward the floor. Bring yourself up to parallel or above parallel as you are able.

A3. V-ups

https://www.youtube.com/embed/EDm1xprt2sY

  • Move under control with core engaged
  • Aim hands to mid calf or above
  • Move arms and legs simultaneously

Core 2

A1. Pre-stretch Crunches with DB

https://www.youtube.com/embed/E3tF_mL21wY

  • Feel a slight stretch in the abs at the initiation of the movement
  • Contract the abs until the back is flat
  • Hold the weight directly above your eyes and aim up for the sky as you contract

A2. 1-arm 1-leg cable row

https://www.youtube.com/embed/hJRCQh02QHI

  • Keep hips level.
  • Resist rotation of the torso and hips as you pull.
  • This exercise may not feel difficult. You won't "feel the burn." That's ok. It's a total core exercise working on balance and core coordination.

A3. Kneeling Half-chop

https://www.youtube.com/embed/X6GjkIVLE9Q

  • Knee closest to the cable column is bent
  • Look at the weight as you pull it across your body

Conditioning 2

150 Shuttle (6x 25m)

https://www.youtube.com/embed/cpAnh4vAmrA

  • Setup 2 cones 25m apart
  • One rep is 6 sprints of 25m length, no rest between sprints
  • You will always do 8 reps total across the two sets, for a total of 1200m of running.
  • Sets change each week, but will typically look like "5,3", ie 5 reps in set 1, 3 reps in set 2. So in set 1 you would do 5 reps = 5 x ( 6x25m, followed by 60sec rest)
